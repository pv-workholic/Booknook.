Highlighted Lessons

Fragments of wisdom collected from stories and poetry.

LESSON 01

People Leave Quietly

Not every goodbye is dramatic. Some people fade in small ways first — shorter conversations, delayed replies, softer laughter. Books taught me to notice absence before silence.

LESSON 02

Healing Isn’t Linear

Some chapters feel lighter than others. Growth comes in waves, setbacks, and repeated lessons. Fiction taught me that becoming whole is rarely graceful.

LESSON 03

Loneliness Has A Voice

The best writers make loneliness feel visible. Through characters, I learned how deeply people want to be understood — even when they pretend otherwise.

LESSON 04

Soft Hearts Survive Too

Strength is often mistaken for hardness. But literature repeatedly proves that tenderness, empathy, and emotional honesty can carry people through impossible things.

Books That Shifted Me

The stories that quietly changed how I think.

The Year I Learned Silence

Some stories taught me that silence can be comfort, distance, rebellion, or grief — depending on who is carrying it.

The Year I Romanticised Rain

Poetry made ordinary moments cinematic. Coffee, storms, windows, city lights — everything suddenly felt worth noticing.

The Year I Chose Myself

Female protagonists with messy, unfinished lives taught me that self-respect sometimes means walking away without closure.

The Year I Started Again

The most beautiful endings weren’t happy endings. They were honest endings — and that changed everything.
